Your Curl
Routine

Low porosity · Afro-textured · Goal: big, soft, frizz-free curls
Your Products
Kinky-Curly Come Clean (clarify)
Design Essentials A&A (shampoo)
As I Am CoWash
Mielle Babassu & Mint (deep cond.)
Kinky-Curly Knot Today (leave-in)
Aunt Jackie's Curl La La (cream)
Argan oil
DIY spray bottle (water + leave-in)
Sunday
Wash Day
🧴 Full Wash
Wash
1
Shampoo Design Essentials A&A
Wet your hair thoroughly with warm water first. Apply shampoo to your scalp — not the length of your hair. Massage with your fingertips in circular motions for 2-3 minutes. The lather will clean the lengths as it rinses through. Rinse well with warm water.
⚡ Use Kinky-Curly Come Clean instead on the 1st Sunday of each month to fully reset buildup.
2
Deep condition + Detangle Mielle Babassu & Mint Wide-tooth comb
Apply deep conditioner generously from roots to ends on soaking wet hair. Then while it's still in, divide into 4 sections and detangle with your wide-tooth comb working from ends to roots — this is when you have maximum slip. Once detangled, put on your shower cap and wrap a warm wet towel around your head. Leave for 10-15 minutes. Rinse with cool water.
🔥 The warm towel forces your cuticles open so the conditioner actually penetrates. Don't skip this for low porosity hair.
Styling (on soaking wet hair — don't towel dry first)
3
Paddle brush — define curl clumps Paddle brush
Hair is already detangled. Now use your paddle brush section by section — place at root, drag to ends in one smooth stroke. This is what creates bigger curl clumps.
4
Apply leave-in Kinky-Curly Knot Today
Hair should still be dripping wet. Apply a generous amount section by section, scrunching upward. Don't rake — scrunch. This encourages curl clumping. Make sure every strand is coated.
5
Apply curl cream Aunt Jackie's Curl La La
Apply on top of your leave-in while hair is still very wet. Small amount — emulsify between palms, scrunch into each section upward. Work in sections for even coverage.
💡 Less is more with low porosity hair. Start small and add if needed — never the other way around.
6
Seal with oil Argan oil
2-3 drops only. Rub between palms and lightly scrunch over your hair. This seals all the moisture in.
7
Scrunch + Air dry — hands off 🚫
Scrunch out excess dripping water with a microfiber towel or old soft t-shirt — scrunch upward a few times and stop, don't rub. Then leave your hair completely alone until fully dry. Every touch breaks curl clumps and creates frizz.
✋ No touching. Not even to check. Wait until completely dry before putting your bonnet on.
Night
8
Put on satin bonnet 🎀
Only once fully dry. Every single night — protects your curl pattern, prevents friction damage, keeps moisture in.

The Golden Rules
🔥
Heat on deep condition day, always. Warm towel over shower cap for 10-15 min every Sunday. Without this, products just sit on top of your low porosity hair and do nothing.
✋
Don't touch your hair while it's drying. Every touch breaks curl clumps and creates frizz. This is the #1 cause of frizzy afros.
💧
Always style on soaking wet hair. Not damp — soaking. If your hair isn't dripping when you apply products, add more water first.
⬆️
Scrunch upward, never rake downward. Raking separates curls into individual strands = frizz. Scrunching groups them into clumps = big curls.
📉
Less product than you think. Low porosity hair builds up fast. Start small and add more if needed — never the other way around.
🎀
Bonnet every single night — only when fully dry. Including at mom's. Cotton destroys your curl pattern overnight.
🔄
Clarify once a month. First Sunday — use Kinky-Curly Come Clean instead of Design Essentials. Resets buildup so your hair can absorb moisture again.
⏳
Give it 4-6 weeks. Your hair needs time to adjust. It won't be perfect week one — stick with it.
